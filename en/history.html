History & Culture of Taoyuan

Timeline of Development

Qing Dynasty Era (1683-1895)

Taoyuan was originally called "Taozigyuan" (桃仔園), named for its abundant peach blossoms. In early periods, it was an area of Pingpu indigenous activity. After the Qing Dynasty, Fujian and Guangdong migrants began cultivation, gradually forming settlements. Daxi, located upstream of the Tamsui River, became an important commercial transshipment hub.

Japanese Colonial Period (1895-1945)

Modern infrastructure was established, including railway and road systems. The Baroque architecture style of Daxi Old Street developed during this period, showcasing the commercial prosperity of the time.

Post-War Industrialization (1945-1979)

Industrial zones were established with rapid population growth. The Shihmen Reservoir was completed, providing agricultural irrigation and domestic water supply. Planning for Taoyuan International Airport began.

Modern Development (1980-Present)

Taoyuan International Airport opened, becoming a major gateway for Taiwan. Taoyuan was upgraded to a special municipality with simultaneous development of transportation, industry, and multicultural diversity. A large influx of foreign migrant workers formed the distinctive "Little Southeast Asia" cultural character.

Hakka Culture's Central Role

Taoyuan is one of Taiwan's cities with the largest Hakka population, comprising about 40% of the city's total population. Hakka settlements are widely distributed in Zhongli, Pingzhen, Yangmei, Longtan, and other areas, with traditional festivals such as "Tian Chuan Day" and "Yimin Festival" still deeply influential.

Hakka Mountain Songs

Traditional Hakka mountain songs are still sung in Taoyuan's mountainous areas. The lyrics often describe life, love, and natural scenery, serving as an important carrier of Hakka culture.

Indigo Dyeing Crafts

Daxi's indigo dyeing crafts inherit Hakka traditions, using natural plant dyes. They showcase the skillful hands and wisdom of Hakka women.

Tea Culture

Longtan, Daxi, and other areas produce abundant tea. Hakka tea culture emphasizes the combination of "tea aroma, human warmth, and mountain views," forming a unique tea-tasting culture.

Traditional Festivals

"Tian Chuan Day" is a festival unique to the Hakka people, symbolizing the legendary story of Nuwa mending the sky; "Yimin Festival" commemorates Hakka ancestors who defended their homeland.

Hakka Cuisine

Hakka cuisine can be found throughout Taoyuan, including:

  • Bao Tiao: Chewy rice noodles served with meat sauce and Chinese chives
  • Cai Bao: Traditional pastries filled with shredded radish
  • Lei Cha: A health beverage made by grinding tea leaves, sesame, and peanuts into powder
  • Hakka Stir-fry: A classic dish made with tofu, squid, and shredded pork

Integration of Diverse Ethnic Groups

Besides the Hakka population, Taoyuan is a city where Minnan people, indigenous peoples, new residents, and foreign migrant workers live together.

Zhongzhen New Village

Representing post-war Yunnan-Burma immigrant culture, Yunnan cuisine, crispy buns, rice noodles, and other foods have taken root here, forming a unique dining culture.

Southeast Asian Culture

Industrial zones in Guishan and Pingzhen concentrate large numbers of Southeast Asian migrant workers, forming diverse culinary street scenes with flavors from Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, and other countries.

Indigenous Culture

The Lalashan area is home to the Atayal indigenous people. Traditional weaving and hunting cultures combine with modern tourism.

New Resident Culture

New residents from various countries bring different cultural perspectives, enriching Taoyuan's cultural heritage.

Local Celebrities

Yan Chengxu

Born January 1, 1977 in Taoyuan.

A member of the male idol group F4, he became famous for starring in the romantic comedy drama "Meteor Garden" (playing Dao Ming Si). Starting as a model, he gradually transitioned to singing and acting, becoming one of the most iconic male artists in the Mandarin entertainment industry.

Yani Tseng

Born January 23, 1989 in Guishan District, Taoyuan.

A professional female golfer who has won numerous international tournament championships and held the title of world's number one female golfer. Known as a "Light of Taiwan," she has significant implications for Taiwan's sports development.

→ Wikipedia

Chu Li-lun

Born June 7, 1961 in Bade District, Taoyuan.

An important political figure in Taiwan who has served as Chairman of the Chinese Unification Promotion Party, Taoyuan County Magistrate, and New Taipei City Mayor. Starting from Taoyuan and entering national politics, he has both local roots and national influence.

→ Wikipedia

Vincent Fang

Born January 26, 1969, originally from Fuli Township, Hualien. Moved to Taoyuan with his family at age 5.

A renowned lyricist in Mandarin pop music who has collaborated with artists like Jay Chou to create numerous popular songs. Though born in Hualien, his important formative years and creative beginning are deeply connected to Taoyuan, making him a talent nurtured by the city.
