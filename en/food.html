Taoyuan Local Food

Yunnan Cuisine of Zhongzhen New Village

Hometown flavors brought by Yunnan-Burma immigrants, sour and spicy, refreshing with rich herbal layers, making it Taoyuan's most distinctive international cuisine.

Classic Yunnan Dishes

Pòsū Bāo (Flaky Buns):Crispy outer layer with rich filling, a signature snack of Zhongzhen New Village.

Mǐgàn (Rice Noodles):Chewy rice noodles served with sour and spicy broth and rich toppings.

Liangbàn Mùguā (Papaya Salad):Refreshing appetizer, sour and spicy with the sweetness of papaya.

Lemongrass Dishes:Seasoned with fresh lemongrass, rich in aroma.

Recommended Restaurants

A-Mei Rice Noodles:Long-established Yunnan restaurant with rich broth

Zhongzhen New Village Market:Multiple Yunnan snack stalls with diverse choices

Yunnan Restaurant:Authentic Yunnan-Burma flavors in a comfortable environment

Dining Information

Best Time:Lunch hours (11:30-14:00)

Average Cost:NT$100-200 per person

Specialty:Experience Yunnan-Burma culture and history

Shihmen Reservoir "Three Ways Live Fish"

Freshly prepared live fish, common combinations include: steamed, sweet and sour, miso soup, best shared with a group.

Live Fish Dish Features

Steamed Fish:Maintains the fresh sweetness of fish, served with ginger and scallions

Sweet and Sour Fish:Sour and sweet appetizing, perfect with rice

Miso Fish Soup:Rich broth, warming and nutritious

Other Options:Braised, salt and pepper, garlic flavors

Recommended Restaurants

Shihmen Live Fish Street:Multiple live fish restaurants gathered, diverse choices

Grandma's Live Fish:Long-established restaurant with traditional flavors

Reservoir Live Fish:Beautiful environment, enjoy lake views while dining

Dining Suggestions

Best Time:Dinner hours, can enjoy sunset

Recommended Group Size:4-8 people, can try various flavors

Average Cost:NT$300-500 per person

Hakka Food

Taoyuan has a large Hakka population, and Hakka food can be found everywhere, showcasing deep cultural heritage.

Bǎn Tiáo (Flat Noodles)

Chewy rice noodles served with minced meat and chives, a classic Hakka staple.

Recommended:Zhongli Flat Noodle Street

Cài Bāo (Vegetable Buns)

Traditional snack with shredded radish filling, soft outer layer with sweet filling.

Recommended:Daxi Old Street

Léi Chá (Ground Tea)

Health drink made by grinding tea leaves, sesame, and peanuts into powder, rich in aroma.

Recommended:Longtan Tea Country

Local Snacks

Each district in Taoyuan has representative local snacks, showcasing regional characteristics.

Daxi Dried Tofu

Signature food of Daxi Old Street, chewy texture with various flavor options.

Recommended Shops:Huang Rixiang, Wanlixiang

Specialty:Traditional handmade, unique texture

Peanut Candy

Traditional sweet of Daxi Old Street, crispy and delicious, a top choice for souvenirs.

Recommended Shop:Old Street Peanut Candy

Specialty:Handmade, no preservatives added

International Cuisine

Taoyuan's "Little Southeast Asia" characteristic makes international cuisine an important part of the city's food scene.

Southeast Asian Cuisine

Industrial areas like Guishan and Pingzhen gather large numbers of Southeast Asian migrant workers, forming rich international food streets.

Vietnamese:Pho, spring rolls, Vietnamese coffee

Thai:Pad Thai, green curry, mango sticky rice

Indonesian:Nasi goreng, satay, nasi lemak

Other International Cuisine

Around Taoyuan International Airport, there are also various international cuisines to satisfy different tastes.

Japanese:Sushi, ramen, izakaya

Korean:Korean BBQ, bibimbap

Western:Italian pasta, American burgers
